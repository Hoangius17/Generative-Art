Don’t litter the
ocean. Protect the
lives living under it.

Marine biologist Sylvia Earle’s quote, “No water, no life. No blue, no green,” perfectly captures what UN Sustainable Development Goal 14 - Life Below Water is all about. Every living thing on Earth is connected to the ocean. The “blue” of the sea gives life to the “green” of the land, without healthy oceans, there would be no balance, no food, and no future for people or the planet.

Yet, our oceans are struggling under the weight of pollution, overfishing, and climate change. Earle’s message is a wake-up call for everyone: Don’t litter the ocean. Protect the lives living under it. If we take care of the ocean, it will continue to take care of us.

Deadly Seigaiha

SDG14 | Life Below Water-Conserve and
sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development.

Concept

We draw inspiration from the circle - the elemental shape that forms every bubble. This simple geometry echoes breath, life cycles, and the underwater world. It is the smallest unit of form that compose everything on the planet yet the biggest form that the galaxy resembles.

I take great inspiration from ocean and Japanese wave pattern - Seigaiha. In this case, there are all normal waves but some of dangered ones showing what is pouring in the ocean - chemicals, oil, fertilizers.

Seigaiha pattern represents peacefulness and long-lasting life. From that, I want to show such contrast of human destruction to highlight the unhealthy and dangerous, urgent situation of the globe in modern life. Make use of the red color to add storytelling and provoke thoughts from observer to artpiece.

Beautiful ocean is what gives us life, we need to protect it.
